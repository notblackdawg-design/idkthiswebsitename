## 2\. What Information We Collect

### 2.1 Information You Provide Directly

**If you create an account:**

* Email address (required for account creation and verification)
* Display name or username (chosen by you, 3–20 characters)
* Profile picture (uploaded voluntarily, optional)
* Bio (optional, max 150 characters)
* Pronouns (optional)
* External platform links such as GitHub, YouTube, Instagram, LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Discord, or a personal website URL (all optional)

**Content you create:**

* Posts including titles, descriptions, tags, and any media files (images or videos) you upload
* Comments you leave on posts
* Reports you submit about other users or content

**If you remain anonymous (no account):**

* You are not required to provide any personal information to post or comment
* A randomly generated Guest ID (a UUID) is created and stored in your browser's localStorage the first time you post
* This Guest ID is never visible to you, other users, or anyone else — it is used only internally to track your posts for rate limiting and to allow you to delete your own content within the same browser

### 2.2 Information Collected Automatically

* **IP address** — collected for rate limiting, abuse prevention, and security purposes. We do not use IP addresses for advertising or sell them to third parties.
* **Browser and device information** — basic technical information such as browser type and operating system, collected passively when you use the platform
* **Session tokens** — generated by our authentication provider (Supabase) when you log in, stored securely in your browser

### 2.3 Information We Do Not Collect

We do not collect:

* Phone numbers
* Mailing or physical addresses
* Payment or billing information
* Date of birth or age (beyond confirming you are 13 or older)
* Real name (your display name is chosen by you and does not need to be your real name)
* Location data beyond your IP address
* Contacts or camera access
* Any data through advertising networks, Facebook Pixel, or retargeting tools

## 4\. Third-Party Services That Receive Your Data

Buildboard uses the following third-party services. By using Buildboard, you acknowledge that your data may be processed by these services as described below.

### 4.1 Supabase

Supabase is our database, file storage, and authentication provider. All user data — including your email address, display name, posts, comments, profile picture, and session tokens — is stored on Supabase's servers. Supabase is SOC 2 Type 2 compliant and processes data in accordance with their own privacy policy available at supabase.com/privacy.

### 4.2 Google Gemini API

When you or another user clicks the "Explain This" button on a post, the post's title and description are sent to Google's Gemini API to generate a plain-language explanation. No personal identifying information is sent — only the post content itself. Google processes this data in accordance with their API Terms of Service and Privacy Policy.

### 

### 4.3 OpenAI Moderation API



Every post title, post description, comment, and uploaded image submitted to Buildboard is automatically sent to OpenAI's Moderation API before being saved. This is to detect and prevent toxic, hateful, harmful, or sexually explicit content. Only the content itself is sent — no account information, email, or personal details are included. OpenAI processes this data in accordance with their API Terms of Service and Privacy Policy.---

## 5\. Cookies and Local Storage

### 5.1 What We Store in Your Browser

|Item|Type|Purpose|Duration|
|-|-|-|-|
|Theme preference (light/dark)|localStorage|Remembers your display preference|Until you clear browser data|
|Guest ID|localStorage|Tracks anonymous posts for rate limiting and deletion|Until you clear browser data|
|Session token|Cookie (Supabase)|Keeps you logged in|7 days or until logout|

### 5.2 What We Do Not Use

We do not use advertising cookies, tracking cookies, third-party analytics cookies, or the Facebook Pixel. We do not use fingerprinting or any cross-site tracking technology.

### 5.3 How to Clear Your Data

You can clear all locally stored data at any time by clearing your browser's cookies and localStorage through your browser settings. Note that clearing your Guest ID means you will lose the ability to delete your anonymous posts.

## 9\. Data Security

We take reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect your data, including:

* All data transmitted between your browser and our servers is encrypted using HTTPS/TLS
* Passwords are never stored in plain text — they are hashed by Supabase Auth
* Database access is protected by Row Level Security (RLS) policies so users can only access their own data
* Media files are stored in private Supabase Storage buckets served via signed URLs
* API keys and sensitive credentials are stored as server-side environment secrets, never exposed to the client

However, no system is completely secure. In the event of a data breach that affects your personal information, we will notify affected users by email within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach, in accordance with GDPR requirements.
